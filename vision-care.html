Ocular Disease Management

From cataracts and diabetic retinopathy to dry eye syndrome and glaucoma, rely on our doctors to treat, co-manage and provide routine monitoring to help maintain your vision for the long-term.

COMMON CONDITIONS

Conditions include AMD, glaucoma, cataracts, diabetic retinopathy, retinal detachment, dry eye syndrome and eye allergies.

Diabetes is a disease where your body cannot properly digest sugars. There are two types of diabetes (Type I and Type II) and both forms can lead to diabetic retinopathy, a disease that can severely damage the retina resulting in permanent vision loss. Diabetic retinopathy is a serious condition that needs to be regularly monitored by an optometrist and annual eye exams for this condition are covered by OHIP once a year regardless of age.

WHAT TO BRING WITH YOU

Bring your eyeglasses, contact lenses boxes, and OHIP card. Sunglasses are also recommended because the eye drops used during the eye exam make patients more sensitive to light for approximately 2-4 hours after the appointment and more difficult to focus up close.

WHAT WE'LL DO

A dilated fundus exam is where drops are used to enlarge the pupil size of the patient in order to obtain a better view of the retina and the back of the eye. This type of exam is important as it is an effective method to evaluate the posterior eye.

APPOINTMENT COSTS

Our comprehensive eye exams include glasses prescription check, dilated fundus exam, retinal photography. Additional testing also available and would be recommended by the Optometrist at your exam if necessary. Please call our office to inquire.

Emergency Eye Care

Have you had a change in your vision as a result of an accident or injury? If you’re experiencing red eyes, flashes of light or new onset of floaters in your vision, don’t delay - give us a call today.

We will try to accommodate a same-day appointment, or an appointment within 48 hours, with one of our eye doctors. When contacting our office, please inform us about the specifics of your symptoms so that we can serve you better.

Frame and Lens Fitting

Find that perfect pair of glasses with our range of quality, on-trend eyewear. Trust our optical dispensers to recommend the best frames and lens for your everyday lifestyle needs, and help you make any adjustments (or repairs!) for a completely comfortable, customized fit.

HOW LONG DOES AN APPOINTMENT TAKE?

Your appointment can take up to an hour in order for us to help select an ideal frame, discuss your lens choices and options and take all the appropriate measurements necessary to make a comfortable and functional pair of glasses.

WHAT TO BRING WITH YOU

If you are new to our office, please bring your most up to date eyeglass prescription, and your current glasses to the appointment.

WHAT TO EXPECT

Our experienced dispensers will help select an ideal frame and lens choice for your prescription and style needs.

FRAME AND LENS FITTING COSTS

There is no cost for our professional service. Please call our office to book an appointment.

Contact Lens Fitting

Proper lens fitting ensures that your contact lenses are healthy, comfortable and fit your lifestyle. We have options to correct for your distance vision, astigmatism, reading needs and even changing the colour of your eyes. Learn how to properly insert, remove and take care of your contact lenses.

HOW LONG DOES A FITTING TAKE?

A proper fitting could range from 15 minutes for a simple refit to an hour for a new fit with instructions.

WHAT TO BRING WITH YOU

Please bring your contact lens boxes if you are a previous or current wearer.

WHAT TO EXPECT

Our doctors will check the health and fit of your current lenses and/ or recommend lenses that best suit your lifestyle.

If you’ve never worn contact lenses before, we’ll show you how to properly insert and remove the contact lenses, as well as how to appropriately manage your contact lens hygiene. After your fitting, you are often given a pair of contact lenses to try at home and ensure they are the best fit for you.

We fit daily, bi-weekly, and monthly lens replacement options, and can correct for single vision (distance), astigmatism, multifocal, monovision and colour as well. We also offer specialty lenses such as rigid gas permeable (RGP) lenses.

CONTACT LENS FITTING COSTS

Fitting costs varies depending on what type of contact lens is needed. Please call our office to inquire.

Laser Vision Co-Management

We provide both pre-operative screenings and post-operative care for patients who have had laser vision correction. We’ll help you determine if you’re the right candidate for surgery, answer any questions about the procedure, and perform all follow-up visits for a speedy recovery.

A comprehensive eye exam is required to determine if you are a good candidate for laser vision correction. Once you would like to go ahead with the surgery, we’ll refer you to our recommended top laser vision correction centers and will follow up with post-operative care as needed.

Your vision is priceless. Many serious eye conditions don’t have obvious symptoms. Some eye diseases only show symptoms when the condition is advanced and difficult, or even impossible, to treat. A comprehensive eye exam provides the full assurance of vision and eye health that a store sight test or a school vision screening cannot.

At Davisville Eye Care, we evaluate many factors that can affect your vision and eye health. We review your case history, conduct an external and internal exam of your eyes, and measure vision qualities, such as eye movements and coordination , sharpness of vision and peripheral vision. We also evaluate your ability to adjust focus, and to see colour and depth normally.
